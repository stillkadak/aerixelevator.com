Elevator Price in India 2026 — Complete Guide

January 2026 Aerix Elevators 8 min read

Planning to install a lift in your building? One of the first questions every developer, builder, or homeowner asks is: what is the price of an elevator in India? This comprehensive guide covers all lift types — passenger elevators, home lifts, goods lifts, and hospital lifts — with real price ranges for 2026.

1. Passenger Lift Price in India 2026

Passenger elevators are the most commonly installed lifts in India for residential apartments, commercial buildings, offices, and hotels. Here are the key price ranges:

  • Basic Residential Passenger Lift (6–8 persons, G+4): ₹6 Lakh – ₹9 Lakh
  • Commercial Passenger Lift (8–13 persons, G+6): ₹9 Lakh – ₹14 Lakh
  • High-Rise Passenger Elevator (G+10 & above): ₹14 Lakh – ₹25 Lakh
  • Panoramic / Glass Elevator: ₹12 Lakh – ₹30 Lakh

Prices depend on capacity, speed, number of stops, MRL vs. machine room, and cabin finish. GST (18%) is charged extra on all elevator purchases.

2. Home Lift Price in India 2026

Residential home lifts have seen growing demand across Indian cities especially in Delhi NCR, Mumbai, Bangalore, and Hyderabad. Home lift prices in India:

  • Basic Home Lift (2–3 persons, G+2): ₹3.5 Lakh – ₹5 Lakh
  • Standard Home Lift (3–4 persons, G+3): ₹5 Lakh – ₹7 Lakh
  • Premium Glass Home Lift: ₹7 Lakh – ₹12 Lakh

3. Goods Lift / Cargo Elevator Price in India 2026

Goods elevator prices depend heavily on load capacity and travel height:

  • 500kg Goods Lift: ₹5 Lakh – ₹7 Lakh
  • 1000–2000kg Cargo Elevator: ₹8 Lakh – ₹14 Lakh
  • 3000–5000kg Industrial Lift: ₹15 Lakh – ₹25 Lakh

4. Hospital Lift Price in India 2026

Hospital lifts require special features — stretcher compatibility, antibacterial surfaces, ARD systems — which affect pricing:

  • Small Clinic Lift: ₹8 Lakh – ₹11 Lakh
  • Standard Hospital Elevator: ₹11 Lakh – ₹18 Lakh
  • Large Multi-Specialty Hospital Lift: ₹18 Lakh – ₹35 Lakh

5. What Factors Affect Elevator Price in India?

The final elevator price depends on: number of floors/stops, capacity (persons/kg), drive technology (MRL vs machine room, V3F vs traditional), door type (manual vs automatic), cabin finish (basic SS vs luxury), and after-sales warranty and AMC terms.

6. How to Get the Best Elevator Price?

Always get quotes from at least 3 manufacturers. Compare specifications — not just headline prices. Ask specifically about: installation cost, GST, warranty terms, and AMC. Passenger Lift vs Hydraulic Lift — Which is Better for Your Building?

February 2026 Aerix Elevators 7 min read

Confused between a traction passenger lift and a hydraulic lift for your building? This is one of the most common questions builders and developers ask in India. Here is a detailed, expert comparison to help you make the right choice.

What is a Traction (Passenger) Lift?

A traction elevator uses a steel rope, pulley system, and an electric motor (usually V3F drive) to move the cab. This is the most common type of passenger lift installed in apartment buildings, commercial offices, and hotels across India. Modern traction lifts come as MRL (Machine Room Less) to save space.

What is a Hydraulic Lift?

A hydraulic elevator uses a fluid-driven piston to push the cab upward. It requires a hydraulic power unit (HPU) and is typically slower than traction lifts. Hydraulic lifts are best suited for low-rise buildings (G+3 and below) and heavy-load goods elevators.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose a traction (MRL) passenger lift if: your building has 4 or more floors, you need faster travel times, you want lower energy bills, or you're installing in a commercial/hotel/hospital setting where ride quality matters.

Choose a hydraulic lift if: your building has only G+1 or G+2 floors, you have budget constraints, or you need a heavy goods elevator where load capacity matters more than speed.
